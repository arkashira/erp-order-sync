# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning

**Primary (1-line):** erp-order-sync turns the chaos of inbound cannabis orders — PDFs, texts, emailed spreadsheets, Leaflink exports — into clean, validated ERP order lines in under 60 seconds, with zero re-keying.

**Alternatives:**
1. *"The OCR-and-paste killer for cannabis distributors."* — sharp, problem-first, names the manual workflow it destroys.
2. *"Order-entry automation that speaks every buyer's format so your ops team doesn't have to."* — emphasizes format-agnostic ingestion, the core moat.
3. *"From buyer's PO to ERP-ready in one click — METRC-aware, audit-clean."* — leads with compliance, the cannabis-specific wedge.

> **Opinion:** Lead with #1. In cannabis distro the buyer *feels* the re-keying pain daily; abstract "automation" positioning converts ~2x worse than naming the exact manual task you eliminate. Reserve the compliance angle (#3) for the sales call, not the headline — it's a closer, not an opener.

| | **Persona A** | **Persona B** | **Persona C** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Name** | "Ops Manager Olivia" | "Founder-Operator Marcus" | "Controller Priya" |
| **Title** | Distribution Ops Manager | Owner / GM, regional distributor | Finance/Compliance Controller |
| **Company** | Mid-size MSO distributor, $20–80M GMV, 8–25 staff | Single-state craft distributor, $3–15M GMV, 4–10 staff | Multi-state operator, $50M+, 30+ staff |
| **Daily job** | Triages 40–150 inbound orders/day across email, text, Leaflink, phone; assigns data-entry to 2–4 clerks; firefights pricing/SKU mismatches | Wears every hat; personally re-keys orders at night; loses deals to slow turnaround | Reconciles orders→invoices→METRC; chases entry errors that break audits |
| **Core pain** | Re-keying + transcription errors = wrong shipments, chargebacks | No ops headcount; order entry is the bottleneck on growth | Manual entry = compliance/audit risk + month-end pain |

> **Opinion:** Sell to **Olivia first** — she has the pain volume, an internal headcount cost to point at ($3–4k/mo loaded per clerk), and enough authority to pilot without a procurement cycle. Marcus closes fast but churns; Priya is a great expansion lever once you're in.

## Launch Milestones

**D-30 — Build proof & pipeline**
- Lock **3 design partners** (free/discounted) — instrument them for hard metrics (orders processed, time/order, error rate).
- Ship reliable ingestion for the **top 3 formats** (Leaflink PDF export, emailed XLSX, plain-text email).
- Build the 400-account outbound list + 5-touch sequence.
- One live ERP integration end-to-end (pick Distru or Canix).

**D-0 — Public launch**
- Founder LinkedIn launch post + the 38-second demo video.
- Outbound sequence goes live (50/day).
- Design-partner story #1 published.
- Target: **15 demos booked** in launch week.

**D+30 — Convert**
- ≥3 design partners converted to **paid**; ≥2 net-new paid logos.
- Webinar with ERP partner shipped.
- CAC validated against the $180–350 outbound estimate; kill/double-down on channels.

**D+90 — Scale the engine**
- ≥12 paying customers; ≥1 signed ERP referral partnership producing inbound.
- Repeatable 5-touch sequence with >8% reply→demo rate.
- First case study with quantified ROI; introduce annual prepay.

> **North-star:** *orders successfully synced to ERP per week.* It's the one number that proves the product replaces re-keying labor — and it's the leading indicator of both expansion revenue and retention. Revenue follows it.

> **Opinion on the $ target:** $5–7k MRR by D+90 is deliberately modest-but-real. With founder-led outbound and a $450 ACV, the constraint is onboarding/integration time, not demand. Hitting it with <5% churn and a working ERP partnership matters far more than a vanity MRR number — it's what makes the Series-of-distributors land-and-expand motion fundable in Q2.
